Procedure

How a case is heard

Four steps, two parties, one ruling that no single party controls.

I

The complaint

File a grievance

The claimant states the dispute, names the remedy sought, and lays out the facts in up to 600 characters. No deposit is taken, only network fees. The case opens on the public docket awaiting an answer.

II

The answer

The other side responds

A different wallet, never the claimant, files the defense. Submitting the answer is what triggers the hearing. The contract refuses a defense from the claimant, so a case is always heard with two genuine sides.

III

The hearing

The magistrate weighs both

An injection-resistant AI magistrate reads grievance and defense together and rules UPHELD, SPLIT, or DISMISSED with a fault score apportioned to the respondent. Concrete claims outweigh pressure or repetition.

IV

The judgment

Consensus enters it

Every validator re-runs the ruling; the verdict must match exactly and the fault score within tolerance. A backstop then clamps the score into its band, and the judgment is entered on the docket for good.
